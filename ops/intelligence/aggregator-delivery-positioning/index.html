The Correction From Micro-Step 35

Old reading that must not drive final strategy

The best current move is not an offer, run only 8 price edits, keep all offers off. This was safe as a portal-protection gate, but it is not enough for the business objective.

Corrected reading

Offers are part of the final system. The first price packet is only the foundation. The next intelligence layer is to attach the right offer to the right SKU role and timing window.

How The Four Sources Are Used

each source has one job; mixing them creates false confidence
01

Swiggy DATA.xlsx

Actual local volume behavior. It tells which product families customers in this cluster already order in quantity: biryani, dry starters, gravies, breads, and basket formats.

02

Zomato MG Road sheet

Rank-price perception. It shows where customers are comfortable seeing price points by product form. It is not absolute order count.

03

HE current menu

SKU truth and execution surface. It tells current names, prices, portions, platform rows, and whether a row can actually be edited.

04

Aggregator playbook

Offer mechanic library. It tells how to express value: item discount, cart offer, fixed combo, timing group, Gold/One, BOGO, ads, and stack guardrails.

The Correct Positioning Stack

this is the new decision order
01

Volume family

Find where customers already buy. This comes mainly from Swiggy and HE own-order evidence.

02

SKU role

Classify the row: hero main, entry basket, combo, attach, premium pull, or visibility item.

03

Base price

Move price into the demand corridor. This can be a decrease, keep, or small increase.

04

Offer mechanic

Choose the visible discount form: item, cart, fixed combo, daypart, Gold/One, or strike-through value.

05

Timing

Attach the offer to the daypart or buying occasion where volume is missing or where customer intent is high.

06

Proof gate

Check stack, MOV, margin, portal state, portion, and screenshots before the team saves it.

What The Team Should Understand From This UI

  • The final win is not "cheap menu." It is "right base price plus visible deal."
  • Chicken Biryani and selected starters/gravy rows should first move to a cleaner Rs299 price point.
  • Mutton Biryani is the first item-level offer candidate, capped and non-stacking.
  • Lunch cart offers and combo value architecture are mandatory future lanes, not optional extras.
  • Swiggy must be handled after live menu readiness proof because its data is strong but the current execution surface is weaker.
