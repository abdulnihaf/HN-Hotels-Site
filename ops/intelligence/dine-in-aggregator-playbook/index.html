SECTION 1

Per-platform offer grammar

Three platforms · three completely different offer-config systems · why one-size-fits-all doesn't work
The single most important insight

EazyDiner, Zomato Dining, and Swiggy Dineout are NOT comparable platforms. They share a category (table-booking aggregators) but their offer-configuration grammars are fundamentally different. EazyDiner exposes a single X% slider with a 25%-bank-stack auto-attached. Zomato Dining is a 5-tile stack (PRE-BOOK + INSTANT + EXCLUSIVE + SCRATCH + BANK) with time-bound subtitle creative. Swiggy Dineout uses pre-book deposits with cashback layering. The "right offer" on one platform is the wrong offer on another. This is why the recommendations below differ structurally per platform — not by accident.

SECTION 6

Methodology & sources

How the recommendations were derived · honesty preface · data files
Methodology
  • EazyDiner corpus: 956 unique BLR restaurants scraped via Next.js __NEXT_DATA__ across 21 cuisine pages → 566 offer-having (filter: restaurant_offer_pct > 0 or non-standard offer string). Top 50 by X% had detail pages re-scraped for eazypay_details, other_deals, rdv_offer_data, foodie_awards, buffet_deals, has_dynamic_buffet, wallet_config. Conclusion: single-grammar surface — no creative addons (BOGOs, time-bound, free-dish hooks) exist on the platform.
  • Zomato Dining corpus: 221 unique BLR restaurants with has_any_offer = true, all 5 offer-tile fields enumerated (PRE-BOOK / INSTANT / EXCLUSIVE / SCRATCH / BANK) including funded_by markers. Time-bound subtitle scan revealed ~12% of restaurants use validity windows (lunch-only, full-day, etc.) but ZERO use a window past midnight — HE's late-night moat.
  • HE detail-page scrape: HE's actual EazyDiner detail page (res_id 712958) scraped to verify 46 fields → 2 of 8 originally-recommended changes were already correct (subtype + category) and got dropped from the email; 4 new asks emerged (webview_menu_url, photo cap, foodie awards, chain linkage) that the corpus alone wouldn't have surfaced.
  • Cost math: Restaurant-funded burn only. Bank-stack and platform-funded portions are excluded — they cost HE nothing.
  • Customer perception: derived from listing-card display string ("Flat X% + 25% Bank" reads as additive in scan-mode browsing).
Honesty preface
  • The "no creative addons exist" claim on EazyDiner is high-confidence but bounded by the cuisines scraped — Lounge/Bar-and-pub/Multi-cuisine slugs returned 0 results both runs, suggesting EazyDiner mis-named those slugs. Substituted desserts/street-food/fast-food/bakery to reach 956 unique. The 566-restaurant corpus is broad enough that this gap is unlikely to hide creative-addon patterns.
  • Volume-lift estimates ("36% break-even", "top-decile placement = 50× lift") are back-of-envelope. Real lift is testable post-flip via the dine-in aggregator-pulse tracking already deployed (resId 22632449 + outlet 1372737 + EazyDiner 712958 in D1).
  • Swiggy Dineout recommendations have NOT been corpus-validated — they're directionally aligned with the Award Winner Sharief Bhai but should be refined after the 500-corpus extraction runs.
  • The 11 PM–3 AM late-night moat assumption depends on HE's actual operating hours being correctly displayed on Zomato. Currently the dashboard may show different hours; one of the listing fixes asks to ensure 2 PM–3 AM is set.
