SECTION 1

Customer-Facing Offers

22 offer types across both platforms · 7 surgical recommendations
Honesty preface on sourcing

Researched across UrbanPiper's partner-help docs (the most authoritative public source — UrbanPiper is the POS layer that integrates with both Swiggy and Zomato APIs and exposes their underlying offer schemas), Swiggy's Catalyst blog and Partner FAQ, Zomato's policy pages, third-party restaurant-tech guides (Menuviel, Foodiv, Kouzina), and TheMediaAnt for ad-pricing primary data.

  • Swiggy keeps its offer-config UI nearly invisible to the public web. UrbanPiper itself states: "Swiggy doesn't send its share of discount in the Order Relay payload, and the configuration of discounts has to be done at the Swiggy backend directly. You can get in touch with your Swiggy PoC for the same." Translation: Swiggy offer creation is largely PoC-mediated, not fully self-serve like Zomato.
  • Zomato is much better-documented because UrbanPiper's KB enumerates the exact discount schema. Treat that as ground truth for Zomato.
  • Funding-split percentages for "co-funded" offers are commercially confidential — any number quoted here is observed-from-customer-side, not contractual.
  • CTRs/conversion benchmarks in Layer 3 are directional industry estimates, not verified for HE specifically. Treat as planning hypotheses.

Layer 2 · Per-platform deep dive

Zomato Restaurant-partner offer config UrbanPiper-documented, mostly self-serve

The single best public source is UrbanPiper's "Running Discounts on Zomato" KB page. It documents four discount categories mapping to Zomato's underlying offer schema:

  1. Percentage Discount — "Apply a percentage off the total cart value, also typically with a minimum spend condition." Cart-wide, with min-cart-value gate.
  2. Fixed Discount — "Offer a set amount off the total bill, usually when a customer spends a certain minimum." Same shape, ₹-denominated.
  3. Percentage Dish Discount — "Give a percentage discount on a single specific item that you choose. This applies once per order." Item-level, idempotent per cart.
  4. BOGO (Buy One Get One Free) — "When your customer adds eligible items to their cart, an item with lower price will be discounted as a free item." Requires tagging eligible items with offer-bogo.
Configuration fields exposed (any of the above)
Title · Start & End Date · Value (% or ₹) · Minimum Order Value · Timing group (HH:MM + day-of-week) · Single use flag
Funding model — Salt / Pepper terminology
Salt Discount = fully restaurant-sponsored (default).
Pepper Discount = fully Zomato-sponsored. Restaurant pays nothing.
Salt + Pepper = co-funded. Split is negotiated case-by-case via your Zomato PoC; not publicly published.

Zomato Gold is a separate construct. Restaurants opt in for visibility on a paying-member base; in exchange they offer BOGO/percentage benefits to Gold members. Commission can be renegotiated downward in some cases. Restaurant primarily funds the visible discount; Zomato bundles you into Gold marketing.

Stackability warning
Restaurant offer + Gold benefit + Zomato platform coupon can stack within Zomato's stacking rules; the customer sees the best combination. This often eats deeper into your margin than expected — flag in Layer 3.
Swiggy Restaurant-partner offer config PoC-heavy; thin public docs

Swiggy is much less self-serve. UrbanPiper plainly says Swiggy offer config "has to be done at the Swiggy backend directly. You can get in touch with your Swiggy PoC."

Restaurant offer types observed in the wild (so they exist)
Percentage off (10–60%) with max ₹ cap (e.g. "60% off up to ₹120 above ₹149") · Flat ₹ off above min cart (e.g. "₹150 off above ₹500") · BOGO on selected items · Free delivery (badged) — partly Swiggy-funded under Swiggy One; restaurant-funded if you buy "Free Delivery" badge · Time-restricted promos (lunch, late-night)

Markup-price feature is exposed by Swiggy and via UrbanPiper: show inflated MRP next to selling price for perceived deal. Combos are configured as products in your menu, not as a separate offer object.

Configuration UI — the partner app has an "Offers & Ads" module. You choose offer kind, select customer category, select duration. Field-level details should be assumed similar to Zomato but verified with your Swiggy KAM.

Swiggy One — Swiggy's subscription. Members get free delivery >₹149 and up to 30% off at participating restaurants. Restaurant opts in. Funding split is co-funded, but the visible discount comes off your menu — Swiggy contributes via reduced commission or marketing match. Treat the discount as ~70% your cost, ~30% Swiggy's, as a planning estimate (not contractual).

Customer-segment targeting limitation
Swiggy publicly describes a sophisticated internal segmentation engine. But for restaurant-side self-serve targeting, you primarily get: lifecycle status (new/repeat), geo, and platform-managed lapsed-user campaigns. New-user "Try New" promotions are usually Swiggy-funded acquisition spend (Pepper-equivalent).

Layer 3 · 7 surgical offer recommendations for HE

Critical math first. At AOV ₹464 with 24.6% Swiggy commission, your platform cost per order is ₹114. If you add a restaurant-funded 20% discount (₹93), you're paying ₹207 to Swiggy ecosystem on a ₹464 order = 44.6% gross to platform. After food cost (~30% = ₹140), you net ₹117 on a ₹464 order = 25% — and that's BEFORE rent, labour, packaging.

Any blanket >20% restaurant-funded discount drives HE into negative contribution margin per order. All 7 recommendations below respect this hard ceiling.

SECTION 2

Paid Visibility & Ads

17 ad-product types · 4 surgical scenarios · ₹18-28K/mo recommended
Honesty preface on confidence

Both Swiggy and Zomato keep their ads pricing deliberately opaque (no public rate-card; per-restaurant SRFs / manager-driven onboarding). Numbers below are triangulated from policy pages, agency blogs, restaurant-owner reportage, and the Swiggy/Zomato partner-app documentation.

  • Several "products" Zomato lists are essentially the same auction priced under a different SKU (Visit Pack, Dining Visit Pack, AI Video Visit Pack, Off-App Visit Pack, Hyperpure-Credits Visit Pack, Package Visit Pack, Standard Legendary Pack) — all sold by KAMs as bundles wrapping the same CPC/CPV auction.
  • "Visit" attribution = user accesses page OR views the full ad (impression-based, 24h window for Visit Pack, 1h for Dining). You pay even for views that never click — this is the source of "thousands of clicks no conversion" complaints in restaurant reportage.
  • Swiggy Self-Serve Ads has a documented April 2026 issue: defaulting to higher budgets and silent auto-renewal; restaurants charged up to ₹20 lakh in unauthorized fees. Operational rule: manually verify the budget cap and end-date every Monday, never trust auto-renewal.

What is a "Visit"? — read carefully
A "Visit" = user either (a) accesses the Restaurant Page by clicking the ad, OR (b) views the full ad (impression-based, 24h window for standard Visit Pack, 1h for Dining). You pay even for views that never click — source of "4,877 clicks no conversion" complaints in restaurant-owner reportage.

Self-serve sponsored listing: ₹500-1000/day enough to test.

Pressure tactic to know
Zomato has been pushing restaurants to spend ≥5% of platform revenue on ads as a soft requirement for ranking. For HE doing ~₹20K/month on Zomato, that's a ~₹1,000 floor (~₹35/day). Don't let the KAM push you past 10% without proof of incremental orders.
