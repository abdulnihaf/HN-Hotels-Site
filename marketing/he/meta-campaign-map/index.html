Decision Spine

The campaign should not be configured as one generic ad. These five decisions decide what Meta learns and how the next static layer behaves.

Step 01

Choose the learning job

Decide whether Meta should find watchers, direction-clickers, message-senders, or tracked conversion users.

Bucket 1: recognition + direction intent.
Step 02

Constrain geography

Define the HKP Road proximity ring before interests. Location is the strongest truth for this local food problem.

Start tight, expand only with proof.
Step 03

Let creative classify

The video tells Meta who stops for the Hamza name, HKP Road, seating, parcel ease, and familiar food.

Video depth becomes audience logic.
Step 04

Build warm pools

Create audiences from 25%, 50%, 75% viewers, page/IG engagers, direction clickers, and WhatsApp/menu actions.

Set audience shells now.
Step 05

Deploy static loops

Serve one message per intent: map proof, dish proof, offer proof, parcel proof, family proof, or night proof.

Creatives can be designed later.

What must be ready now

Video creative, campaign/ad set naming, location ring, objective, CTA, destination, UTM/action URL, and audience naming convention.

What can wait

Final retargeting static creatives, dish-specific offer cards, late-night creative variants, and budget automation rules.

What should not happen

Do not force cold loyalists into WhatsApp first. The previous CTWA campaign proved message clicks alone do not equal restaurant intent.

 Sets the campaign creation path and the type of behavior Meta will optimize toward. Awareness, Traffic, Engagement, Leads, Sales, App promotion.  The actual result Meta tries to find inside the objective. Reach, impressions, ThruPlay/video views, link clicks, landing page views, messages, calls, conversions. Which signal best proves a local loyalist is warming up without pushing them too early? Use video/engagement for pool creation or traffic if destination is a directions/action page. Now
CTA button
Ad level
 Defines the visible action on the ad and influences user intent quality. Get Directions, Learn More, Send Message, Call Now, See Menu, Order Now where available. Should the loyalist act physically or enter WhatsApp? Cold video: Get Directions. Warm static: Send Message for menu/offer. Hot static: Directions or Call. Now
Destination
Click target
 Where Meta sends the click and what follow-up data can be measured. Google Maps link, website/action page, WhatsApp thread, call, lead form. Do we want map action, menu action, offer action, or conversation action? Prefer trackable action page or map destination for cold. WhatsApp only after warm signal. Now
Location ring
Audience boundary
 Hard geographic eligibility for who can be shown the ad. 1 km, 3 km, 5 km, pin drop, city, exclude areas if needed. Is this person close enough to actually visit HKP Road? Bucket 1 starts tight: HKP/Shivajinagar first, then 3 km if volume is weak. Now
Age and gender
Demographic boundary
 Hard inclusion/exclusion before Meta prediction begins. 18-24, 25-34, 35-44, 45-55; all genders or selected gender. Are we excluding family decision-makers and old Hamza memory by being too narrow? Do not repeat old male-only logic. Bucket 1 should include older/family users. Now
Language
Profile/interface signal
 Limits delivery to users with selected language signals. English, Hindi, Urdu, Kannada, no language restriction. Will language targeting shrink local reach more than it helps? Keep broad. Let Urdu/Dakhni creative filter the right people subconsciously. Risk
Detailed targeting
Interests/behaviors
 Adds interest, demographic, or behavior signals, but these are weaker than real local intent. Food, restaurants, biryani, kebab, street food, dining, delivery apps, local pages where available. Can Meta directly know a Hamza loyalist? No. It can infer from response to creative. Use lightly. Do not over-narrow. Creative response is the real classifier. Risk
Advantage audience
Meta expansion
 Allows Meta more freedom beyond the suggested or original audience settings. On, off, suggested audience, original audience controls where available. Do we trust Meta to expand beyond HKP logic before warm data exists? Use carefully after the first learning cycle. Not the first lever for Bucket 1. Later
Placements
Where ad appears
 Controls surfaces like Instagram Reels, Stories, Feed, Facebook Feed, Messenger, Audience Network. Advantage placements or manual Reels/Stories/Feed selection. Where will a loyalist actually stop and understand the Hamza reset? Use vertical-first creative for Reels/Stories; Feed support for older/family users. Now
Budget type
Campaign or ad set
 Decides whether Meta distributes budget across ad sets or each ad set has its own controlled spend. Campaign budget, ad set budget, daily budget, lifetime budget. Are we learning one bucket cleanly or letting Meta blend signals across many buckets? For Bucket 1, controlled ad set budget is cleaner. Multi-bucket budget comes later. Now
Schedule/dayparting
Time controls
 Controls when ads are eligible to deliver; strict hour scheduling usually needs lifetime budget. Always on, lunch window, evening parcel, dinner, late-night, weekend family slots. Which eater is hungry at which time? Cold video can run broad. Static retargeting should split by food window. Later
Bid strategy
Auction behavior
 Controls how Meta bids for the selected performance goal. Lowest cost, cost cap, bid cap, ROAS-style controls where available. Do we already know the value of a direction click or warm viewer? Start lowest cost. Add caps only after real HE benchmarks exist. Later
Audience shells
Retargeting pools
 Reusable custom audiences that collect people based on engagement, video depth, website events, or lists. 25%, 50%, 75% video viewers; IG/FB engagers; website/menu viewers; WhatsApp clickers. Which viewer response means loyalist, curious, hungry, or ready to act? Create naming convention now; final static creative can come after data. Now
Exclusions
Funnel hygiene
 Prevents the wrong layer from showing to people who moved deeper or should not be contacted. Exclude message senders, offer claimers, DND, staff, recent converters, 75% viewers from 25% layer. Are we wasting impressions by repeating the same cold story to warm people? Use exclusions once warm pools are active. Avoid over-excluding during first learning. Later
Creative format
Video/static/carousel
 Determines what can be shown and how people consume the message. Single video, single image, carousel, existing post, flexible creative, instant experience. What creative format best performs the job: recognition, hunger, proof, or action? Cold: one primary video. Warm: static and carousel. Hot: single direct-action static. Now
Placement customization
Crop and text by surface
 Allows different media/crops/text treatment by Reels, Stories, Feed, and other placements. 9:16 vertical, 1:1 feed, 4:5 feed, story-safe text, placement-specific thumbnails. Does the same creative communicate in three seconds on every surface? Prepare vertical video first; build square/4:5 statics only after signal map is known. Later
Tracking
Measurement layer
 Defines what we can read after launch: URL params, pixel, events, API/offline events. UTM links, short action pages, pixel events, CAPI, offline counter codes. How do we know if a viewer became a visitor, message sender, caller, or counter customer? Use trackable URLs/action routes from day one. Offline codes can follow. Now
Automation rules
Budget and pause logic
 Rules/API can shift budget, pause bad ads, or rotate time-window creatives. Pause by cost, boost by viewer pool, schedule by hour, creative fatigue guard. When should budget move from recognition to hunger/action? Define logic now, automate later after first data cycle. Later
Reporting columns
Judgement system
 Controls what results are visible for decision-making. Reach, frequency, video 25/50/75%, CPC, CTR, profile visits, direction clicks, messages. Will we judge the campaign by cheap clicks or by useful local intent? Judge cost per warm viewer and direction/menu intent, not messages alone. Now

Launch Decision Checklist

These are the one-by-one decisions to lock before opening Ads Manager for the first Bucket 1 campaign.

01

Objective

Choose whether initial campaign optimizes for awareness/video engagement or traffic/direction intent.

02

Destination

Decide direct Maps link vs trackable HE action page that then offers map, menu, and WhatsApp.

03

Location radius

Set initial HKP/Shivajinagar radius. Do not start too wide before local signal quality is known.

04

Age and gender

Include the older loyalist and family decision layer; do not repeat the narrow old CTWA audience.

05

Creative upload

Use one primary video first. If two videos exist, A/B test one variable only.

06

Audience naming

Create exact naming convention for 25%, 50%, 75%, engagers, direction clickers, and WhatsApp clickers.

07

Reporting columns

Prepare columns for video depth, cost per warm viewer, frequency, directions, profile visits, and messages.

08

Static design trigger

Do not design every static now. Design when first 24-72 hour data shows which pools are forming.

Answer to the current execution question: the initial configuration does not require final retargeting static creatives. It requires the video, CTA, destination, location, audience shells, and tracking logic.
Core static-layer rule: one warm signal gets one creative job. Do not show offer, map, family, parcel, and late-night proof in the same static.
