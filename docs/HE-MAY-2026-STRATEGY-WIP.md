## 1. Where we are in the conversation

We had the iPhone chat structure the scattered context for HE's May target.
After loading `data/snapshots/sales-daily-last60d.json` and the marketing
snapshots, the picture became different from what was assumed earlier:

- April HE baseline is ~**₹10,300/day** (not ₹21K/day as March suggested).
- The W12 (Mar 16–22) peak of ₹32,365/day was a religious-season spike,
  not a normal benchmark. Ramadan 2026 ran Feb 18–Mar 19, Eid al-Fitr fell
  Mar 20. W11–W12 was Iftar+Eid demand. W13 onwards is the post-Eid
  baseline. So May, with no major Muslim festival, is climbing from
  ~₹10K/day, not "recovering" to ₹32K.
- **May target is ₹15L = avg ₹48,387/day = ~4.7× current run-rate.** That
  reframing matters. It's not a +127% lift over March — it's nearly 5×
  over April.

## 2. The decision the iPhone chat is parked on

Owner asked for a "very very realistic" ramp where strategies are layered
one at a time, each producing measurable lift, ending May at a daily
run-rate that yields ₹15L total.

The honest read after data review:

- **Realistic May with disciplined one-at-a-time execution: ₹6–7L total.**
  End-of-May daily run-rate ~₹22–28K/day. ₹15L on this trajectory is a
  **July** target, not a May target.
- **Parallelized blitz to chase ₹15L in May:** ₹2–3L marketing spend,
  60–70% likely wasted because conversion infrastructure (CTWA responder,
  Swiggy listing, EazyDiner activation) isn't ready to absorb demand.
  Realistic landing zone even in blitz mode: ₹9–11L.
- **₹15L in May from ~₹10K/day baseline in 31 days, in a non-festival
  month, with no new outlet, is not what restaurants in this locality do.**

**Open decision:** plan toward (a) realistic ₹6–7L May with ₹15L by July
as a 90-day arc, or (b) parallelized blitz at ₹2–3L marketing spend
chasing ₹15L May with risk acknowledged.

When the laptop chat resumes, this is the first question to put back to
the owner.
