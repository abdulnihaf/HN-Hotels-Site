# SOP — Cost Reduction Termination (Retrenchment / Role Elimination)

**Trigger:** You need to reduce headcount for business reasons unrelated to employee performance — declining sales, role no longer needed, restructuring, automation, etc.

**Legal classification:** Retrenchment under Industrial Disputes Act 1947, Section 25F.

**Cost is fixed by formula. There's no legal way to reduce it for non-fault termination of a confirmed employee.**

---

## What's owed (formula)

For each employee being retrenched:

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| **Notice** | 30 days notice OR 30 days wages-in-lieu |
| **Retrenchment compensation** | 15 days' average wage × completed years of service (any portion of year > 6 months counts as full year) |
| **Earned wages** | Up to last working day |
| **Leave encashment** | Per Karnataka Shops Act |
| **Statutory bonus** | If applicable, pro-rata |
| **Retention bonus** | Per contract terms — usually paid since this isn't employee fault |

## Worked examples

**Anthony — Washer at HE, ₹18,000 wage, 1.5 years tenure:**
- Average daily wage: ₹600
- Notice: 30 × ₹600 = **₹18,000**
- Retrenchment comp: 15 × ₹600 × 2 (years rounded up) = **₹18,000**
- Earned base wages + leave + bonus: variable, depends on month
- **Total exit cost: ~₹36,000-40,000**

**Mujib — Tea Master at NCH, ₹45,000 wage, 2 years tenure:**
- Average daily wage: ₹1,500
- Notice: 30 × ₹1,500 = **₹45,000**
- Retrenchment comp: 15 × ₹1,500 × 2 = **₹45,000**
- **Total exit cost: ~₹90,000-100,000**

This is non-negotiable for confirmed employees. Plan accordingly.

## Minimum legal timeline — three options

| Option | Day-0 cost | Last day | Process |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Pay & exit immediately** | Notice wages + retrenchment comp + earned dues | Same day | Notice + payment delivered together; employee leaves Day 0 |
| **B. Notice route** | Earned wages during notice + retrenchment comp on last day | Day 30 | Written notice on Day 0; employee continues for 30 days |
| **C. Mutual separation (VSS)** | Negotiated lump sum (typically 2-3 months wages) | Same day | Employee voluntarily resigns in exchange for goodwill payment |

**Option C is recommended when budget allows** because:
- Cleaner paperwork (no Form Q to government)
- No "Last in, first out" obligation
- No 12-month reinstatement obligation
- Employee usually prefers it (gets cash + can claim resignation in references)
- Both parties walk away aligned

## Procedure for Option B (Notice Route)

### Day 0 — Decision and Documentation

- Document business reason for retrenchment in a board / management minute:
  - "Sales for HE decreased by X%"
  - "Role of [position] eliminated due to operational restructuring"
  - "Hours per week reduced from Y to Z, requiring fewer staff"
- Identify employees per "Last in, first out" within the same role unless documented exception
- Calculate exact dues for each retrenchee

### Day 1 — Notice and Form Q

**Issue Notice of Retrenchment** to each employee:

```
HN HOTELS PRIVATE LIMITED
[Letterhead]

Date: __________

To: [Employee Name]
[Role, Establishment]

Subject: Notice of Retrenchment

Dear [Name],

The Company has, after careful consideration, decided to discontinue the role
of [position] at [establishment] with effect from __________ (Day 30 from this
notice) due to [stated business reason].

This notice is issued under Section 25F of the Industrial Disputes Act 1947
read with Section 3 of the Service Rules dated [signing date].

Statutory entitlements payable to you on the last working day:

  Notice period (30 days, served per this notice):     Earned wages during notice
  Retrenchment compensation:                            ₹ [15 days × yrs × daily wage]
  Earned base wages up to last day:                     ₹ ____
  Leave encashment:                                     ₹ ____
  Pro-rated statutory bonus:                            ₹ ____
  Retention Bonus pro-rata:                             ₹ ____
  Total:                                                ₹ ____

Final Settlement Statement will be issued and amount transferred within 7 days
of last working day.

ESI and EPF accumulations will be processed via the respective portals.

The Company shall, for a period of 12 months from your retrenchment date, give
you preference for re-employment if a suitable vacancy arises in the same
classification (per Section 25H of ID Act 1947).

We thank you for your service.

Regards,

Abdul Khader Nihaf
Managing Director
HN Hotels Private Limited
DIN: 08387440
```

**File Form Q** with the Karnataka Labour Department within 30 days of retrenchment date. Available online at sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in or by hand at Karmika Bhavana office.

### Day 2-29 — Notice Period

- Employee continues to work in their role
- Standard payroll continues
- Maintain professional environment — no harassment, no demotion
- Allow them time to interview elsewhere

### Day 30 — Last Working Day

- Conduct exit interview
- Collect company property (uniform, badge, biometric removed)
- Issue **Final Settlement Statement**
- Transfer net amount within 7 days
- Issue **Relieving Letter** + **Experience Certificate** (no negative remarks)

### Day 30+ — Post-exit obligations

- ESIC: mark Date of Exit in IP record
- EPF: process settlement claim if member (not applicable for HN's excluded employees)
- Form Q: confirm filed, retain copy
- For 12 months: maintain a roster of retrenched employees. If a similar vacancy opens, send written offer to retrenched person FIRST.

## Procedure for Option C (Mutual Separation / VSS)

### Day 0 — Offer

Approach employee privately:

> "We're going through a difficult period and need to reduce headcount. I'd like to offer you a voluntary separation package: [3 months' wage] as goodwill payment, paid immediately, in exchange for your written resignation. This is in addition to your earned dues. You're free to take a few days to decide."

### Day 0-3 — Negotiation Window

- Allow employee to consult family
- Be willing to adjust the goodwill amount (within reason)
- Don't pressure — coercion makes the agreement voidable

### Day 3 — Agreement

If accepted, draft and sign:

**Mutual Separation Agreement:**

```
This Mutual Separation Agreement is made between:

HN Hotels Private Limited (Employer)
AND
[Employee Name], [Role, Establishment] (Employee)

Whereas the parties wish to terminate the employment relationship by mutual consent,
the parties agree as follows:

1. Last working day: __________ (today)
2. The Employer shall pay to the Employee, within 7 days:
   a. Earned base wages: ₹ ____
   b. Leave encashment: ₹ ____
   c. Pro-rata statutory bonus: ₹ ____
   d. Goodwill payment (settlement): ₹ ____
   Total: ₹ ____
3. The Employee shall return all Company property today.
4. The Employee acknowledges that the Goodwill payment is in full and final
   settlement of any and all claims arising from the employment relationship,
   including but not limited to: notice period, retrenchment compensation,
   wrongful termination, gratuity (where applicable), and any other statutory
   or contractual claim.
5. The Employee waives the right to sue the Employer in any forum on any matter
   relating to the employment, except for claims under non-waivable statutes
   (e.g., POSH, statutory minimum wage shortfall).
6. The Employer shall provide a positive Relieving Letter and Experience
   Certificate stating the Employee resigned voluntarily.
7. Confidentiality and non-solicitation obligations under the original
   Service Rules continue to apply.
8. Both parties confirm they enter this Agreement freely and voluntarily.

Employee:                           Employer:

________________________            ________________________
[Name]                              Abdul Khader Nihaf
Date:                               Managing Director, DIN: 08387440
                                    HN Hotels Pvt Ltd

Witness:                            Date:

________________________
[Name, Phone]
```

### Day 3 — Execution

- Pay the agreed amount immediately (bank transfer)
- Issue Relieving Letter
- Update CAMS / Odoo / D1
- File the Mutual Separation Agreement in the employee's record

### Why VSS is cheaper despite the goodwill payment

For Anthony (1.5 yr tenure):
- Statutory retrenchment: ₹36,000-40,000 + Form Q + LIFO + 12-month reinstatement risk
- VSS: ₹54,000 (3 months wage) + earned dues, NO Form Q, NO reinstatement, NO LIFO

The marginal ₹15-20K difference buys you operational flexibility worth more.

## What you cannot do

| Shortcut | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| "Layoff" without compensation | "Layoff" in Indian law = temporary suspension with 50% wage. Cannot be permanent without 25F retrenchment. |
| Force resignation by reducing duties | Constructive dismissal claim — costs more than retrenchment |
| Make role redundant + create same role under different title | Sham retrenchment — court reinstates with back wages |
| Skip Form Q for "small numbers" | Filing is mandatory regardless of count |
| Re-hire someone else for same role within 12 months without offering retrenched employee | Section 25H violation; wage liability for retrenched employee from rehire date |

## Decision matrix

When you're considering reducing headcount:

```
1. Is the role genuinely no longer needed?
   YES → proceed with retrenchment SOP
   NO → reconsider; can the role be reassigned?

2. Is the employee on probation (< 6 months service)?
   YES → terminate per probation clause (15 days notice or wages in lieu, no retrenchment comp)
   NO → full retrenchment cost applies

3. Can you afford 1.5 months wages × number of people?
   YES → Option B (Notice route) is cheapest pure-statutory path
   NO → Option C (VSS) is cleaner but slightly more expensive
   STRESSED → wait until cashflow improves; meanwhile reduce variable pay (within contract)

4. How urgent is the exit?
   IMMEDIATE → Option C (VSS) — same day exit possible
   30+ DAYS OK → Option B
   60+ DAYS → Option B with cleaner messaging window

5. Risk of labour disputes?
   LOW (small staff, no union) → Option B is safe
   MEDIUM (unhappy departure) → Option C with broader waiver
   HIGH → involve a labour-law advocate
```

## Owner's pre-flight checklist

Before invoking any cost-reduction termination:

- [ ] Business case documented in writing (sales decline, role redundancy, etc.)
- [ ] Cash budget for exit costs available (1.5-3 months' wage × number of people)
- [ ] LIFO ranking calculated within affected role category
- [ ] Decision sign-off in writing (board minute or management memo)
- [ ] Employee selection criteria objective and documented
- [ ] Communication script prepared (private, respectful)
- [ ] Form Q template ready (for Option B)
- [ ] Mutual Separation Agreement template ready (for Option C)
- [ ] Final Settlement calculator validated
