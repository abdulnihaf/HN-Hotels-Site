### 3. Notice Period (Mutual)

a) Either party may terminate this engagement by giving **30 days' notice in writing**, addressed to the other party.

b) Either party may, in lieu of notice, pay an amount equal to **30 days' gross wage** to the other party.

c) **Where Employer terminates** for reasons not amounting to misconduct: 30-day notice or 30-day wages in lieu, plus retrenchment compensation if applicable per ID Act 1947.

d) **Where Employer terminates for misconduct** (per Section 4 below): no notice required; immediate dismissal upon written order.

e) **Where Employee resigns:** 30-day notice in writing. Notice period must be served unless waived in writing by the Company.

f) **Failure by Employee to serve notice:**
   - All earned base wages will be paid as required by Payment of Wages Act 1936 within 7 days of last working day.
   - Any unpaid Retention Bonus (per Section 9 below) shall stand forfeited.
   - Any company property in your possession (uniform, badge, equipment, cash float, keys) must be returned; cost of unreturned items will be deducted from final dues at fair value, with notice as required by Section 10 of Payment of Wages Act.
   - The Company reserves all legal remedies under Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 73, for any provable loss caused by abrupt exit.

### 4. Code of Conduct — Misconduct Definitions

The following acts constitute **major misconduct** warranting immediate dismissal upon proof through domestic enquiry, without notice or wages-in-lieu, in accordance with Section 39 of Karnataka Shops & Commercial Establishments Act 1961:

a) **Theft, fraud, dishonesty** in connection with the Employer's business — including but not limited to: pilferage of cash, food, supplies, raw materials; manipulation of POS or vendor invoices; false expense claims; ghost employee or kickback arrangements.

b) **Wilful insubordination** or disobedience of any lawful and reasonable instruction of a superior.

c) **Habitual absence without leave** — defined as 3 or more unauthorised absences in any 30-day period, or 5+ in any 90-day period.

d) **Habitual late attendance** — defined as 5+ instances of late punch-in beyond 15 minutes without prior approval, in any 30-day period.

e) **Drunkenness, riotous, disorderly or indecent behaviour** on the Employer's premises.

f) **Wilful damage to** or **loss of** Employer's property.

g) **Disclosure of confidential information** to competitors, customers, suppliers, or any third party without authorisation. Confidential information includes: recipes, vendor pricing, customer data, employee compensation, financial information, business plans.

h) **Sexual harassment** of any co-worker, customer, vendor, or visitor. Such cases are referred to the Internal Committee under POSH Act 2013 in addition to internal action.

i) **Engaging in any other employment** for monetary gain during working hours without written permission of the Company.

j) **Acceptance of any commission, gift, or gratuity** from a vendor, customer, or third party in connection with Employer's business, beyond customary tips.

k) **Conviction by a court** of any offence involving moral turpitude.

l) **Falsification of personal information** (Aadhaar, qualifications, prior employment) at the time of joining or thereafter.

m) **Smoking on premises** in violation of FSSAI regulations and the Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products Act 2003.

The Company reserves the right to declare an act misconduct even if not enumerated above, provided it is materially injurious to the business and is so notified in writing to the employee.

### 5. Domestic Enquiry Procedure for Misconduct

Where misconduct is alleged:

a) **Charge-sheet:** Employee shall be served a written charge-sheet specifying the act of misconduct, date, time, and the clause violated. A response in writing within 3 working days shall be required.

b) **Suspension pending enquiry:** Employee may be placed under suspension during the enquiry, with subsistence allowance payable per Karnataka Payment of Subsistence Allowance Act 1988: **50% of wages (basic + DA) for first 90 days, 75% for days 91-180, 90% beyond 180 days**. Subsistence is paid through the regular monthly pay cycle. Suspension exceeding 6 months without conclusion of enquiry will be considered for review.

c) **Enquiry Officer:** A neutral person not directly involved in the matter shall conduct the enquiry. Employee shall have the right to be assisted by a co-worker as defence representative.

d) **Hearing:** Witnesses shall be examined and may be cross-examined by the employee. Documentary evidence shall be marked and shown.

e) **Findings:** Enquiry Officer shall submit a written findings report to the Managing Director.

f) **Order:** If findings establish misconduct, the Managing Director shall pass a written dismissal order specifying the misconduct established and the statutory basis. The order shall be served on the employee.

g) **Final Settlement:** All earned dues shall be paid within **2 working days** of the last working day per Karnataka Code on Wages Rules 2024. Provable loss attributable to the misconduct (per Section 10 of Payment of Wages Act 1936) shall be deducted from final dues. Any forfeitable amounts (Retention Bonus) stand forfeited.

h) **Gratuity forfeiture (where service ≥ 5 years):** Where the misconduct constitutes an offence involving moral turpitude or wilful damage / loss / destruction of Company property, gratuity payable under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972 may be forfeited to the extent of the loss caused, in accordance with Section 4(6) of the said Act. Such forfeiture shall be explicitly invoked in the dismissal order with quantification of loss.

h) **Criminal action:** The Company reserves the right to file police complaint or initiate civil suit independently of the dismissal action.

### 6. Performance Standards and Improvement Plan

a) Each employee shall be expected to perform the duties of their role to a reasonable standard of competence, attendance, and conduct.

b) **Performance review:** Conducted at minimum once per quarter through a documented framework based on attendance, productivity, customer feedback, and adherence to operating procedures.

c) **Underperformance procedure:**
   - Step 1 — **Verbal counselling**, noted in employee file.
   - Step 2 — **Written warning** with specific deficiencies, expected standards, and a 14-day improvement window.
   - Step 3 — **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** of 30 days with stated KPIs, daily/weekly check-ins, and consequences for failure.
   - Step 4 — **Termination upon failed PIP**, with 30-day notice or wages in lieu per Section 3 above.

d) Failure to clear a PIP, after a written warning, shall constitute "reasonable cause" within the meaning of Section 39 of the Karnataka Shops & Commercial Establishments Act 1961.

e) During the PIP, the Company may reassign duties, withhold variable pay components, and require additional training. Base wage shall not be reduced during the PIP.

### 7. Custodial Responsibility for Cash and Property

Where your role involves handling cash, inventory, or company property:

a) You acknowledge personal custody of the cash float / inventory / equipment assigned to you.

b) Daily reconciliation must be performed and signed off in the cash register / inventory log.

c) **Any unexplained cash short or inventory loss** during your shift may be:
   - Investigated by the Company within 7 days
   - Recovered from your final dues at actual loss value, with prior written notice (per Section 10 of Payment of Wages Act 1936)
   - Treated as misconduct under Section 4(a) above if intent or negligence is established by enquiry

d) You are responsible for the safekeeping of any uniform, key, badge, biometric access, or equipment issued to you. Unreturned items at exit will be deducted from final dues at fair replacement cost.

### 8. Probation (for Future Hires)

a) Where this letter is issued at the time of joining, employment shall be on probation for an initial period of **6 months** from the date of joining.

b) Probation may be extended **once**, by a maximum of **3 months**, by written notice with reasons recorded. No further extension shall be permitted; in the absence of timely confirmation or extension, you shall be deemed confirmed.

c) During probation, either party may terminate with **15 days' notice** or wages in lieu, without obligation of retrenchment compensation under Section 25F of the Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (which applies only after 240 days of continuous service).

d) During probation, performance shall be assessed against the duties of the role. Unsatisfactory performance during probation may result in non-confirmation and termination per (c) above. Where probation termination involves any allegation of misconduct or stigma, the procedure under Section 5 above shall apply notwithstanding probation status (per *Chaitanya Prakash v. H. Omkarappa*, 2010 SC).

e) Confirmation of employment shall be communicated in writing at the end of probation.

> *For employees signing this annexure who have already completed more than 6 months of continuous service: clause 8 does not apply retrospectively. You are deemed confirmed.*

### 9. Variable Pay and Retention Bonus

For consistency with Karnataka labour practice and to recognise stable service:

a) The Company may, from time to time, declare a **Retention Bonus** payable in addition to monthly wage. The amount, frequency, and conditions shall be notified in writing.

b) Retention Bonus is **conditional on continuance in service** through the qualifying date. An employee who exits before the qualifying date — whether by resignation without serving full notice, or by dismissal for misconduct, or by abandonment — shall **forfeit** the Retention Bonus pro-rata.

c) Variable pay components linked to performance metrics (e.g., monthly attendance bonus, customer satisfaction bonus) are payable only on demonstration of the relevant metric in the qualifying period.

d) Statutory bonus under Payment of Bonus Act 1965 (where applicable) shall be paid as required by law and is separate from the Retention Bonus mechanism above.

### 10. Confidentiality and Non-Solicitation

a) **Confidentiality:** During and after employment, you shall not disclose to any third party (including but not limited to: competitors, vendors, customers, social media, journalists) any of the Company's confidential information including recipes, vendor pricing, customer data, employee compensation, financial information, expansion plans, or business strategies. This obligation survives the termination of employment for **3 years**.

b) **Non-solicitation:** During employment and for **6 months** after exit, you shall not solicit any of the Company's employees or vendors to leave the Company or breach their contractual relationship with the Company.

c) **Return of materials:** At exit, you shall return all Company property and not retain any copies (physical or digital) of confidential information.
