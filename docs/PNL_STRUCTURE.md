# ARCHITECTURE: 4-Level Hierarchy

```
Level 1: GROUP (HN)
  Level 2: BRAND (Hamza Express / Nawabi Chai House)
    Level 3: OUTLET (each physical location)
      Level 4: CHANNEL (Dine-in / Takeaway / Zomato / Swiggy / Direct Delivery / Catering)
```

Every number flows through this hierarchy. A single biryani order on Swiggy at the Koramangala outlet rolls up from Channel → Outlet → Brand → Group.

# LEAKAGE DETECTION FRAMEWORK

## Where money disappears without you knowing:

### 1. Portion Control Drift
- **Signal**: Actual food cost > Theoretical food cost by >3%
- **Root cause**: Cooks over-portioning, no standard recipes
- **Fix**: Recipe cards with gram weights, calibrated scoops, spot checks

### 2. Theft & Pilferage
- **Signal**: Inventory shrinkage >3%, unexplained stock-outs
- **Root cause**: Staff taking raw materials, unrecorded sales
- **Fix**: CCTV, daily inventory counts on high-value items (chicken, oil, cheese), POS void tracking

### 3. Aggregator Reconciliation Gaps
- **Signal**: Payout < expected by >2%
- **Root cause**: Unverified refund deductions, commission on GST-inclusive amounts, missing orders
- **Fix**: Weekly line-by-line reconciliation, dispute unverified refunds within 7 days

### 4. Energy Waste
- **Signal**: Utility bills >5% of revenue
- **Root cause**: Equipment running during off-peak, inefficient refrigeration
- **Fix**: Equipment checklists, timer switches, energy audit

### 5. Over-Ordering
- **Signal**: Spoilage >3%, dead stock accumulation
- **Root cause**: No par levels, no purchase orders tied to sales forecast
- **Fix**: Par level system, purchase orders, FIFO enforcement

### 6. Discount Leakage
- **Signal**: Unexplained margin erosion on specific items
- **Root cause**: Staff giving unauthorized discounts, aggregator restaurant-funded promos not tracked
- **Fix**: Discount approval workflow, weekly promo cost analysis

### 7. Cash Leakage
- **Signal**: Cash register short by >Rs 100/day
- **Root cause**: Theft, unrecorded transactions, incorrect change
- **Fix**: Daily cash reconciliation, surprise audits, digital payment push

# GST IMPACT — THE HIDDEN COST

At 5% GST (no ITC), every input GST you pay is a dead cost:

| Input | GST Rate | Monthly Spend | Dead GST Cost |
|-------|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Rent | 18% | 60,000 | 10,800 |
| Equipment repairs | 18% | 10,000 | 1,800 |
| Software/POS | 18% | 5,000 | 900 |
| Professional services | 18% | 5,000 | 900 |
| Raw materials (various) | 5-18% | 3,00,000 | ~15,000-30,000 |
| **Total dead GST/month** | | | **~30,000-45,000** |

This is Rs 3.6-5.4 lakh/year in GST you pay but cannot recover. Factor this into pricing.

**Exception**: GST on aggregator commissions (18%) IS claimable as ITC. On Rs 1L commission, you save Rs 18,000 in ITC.

# MENU ENGINEERING MATRIX

Classify every menu item quarterly:

```
                    HIGH PROFITABILITY
                         |
         PUZZLES         |        STARS
      (Market more)      |     (Promote heavily)
                         |
  LOW POPULARITY --------+-------- HIGH POPULARITY
                         |
          DOGS           |      PLOWHORSES
      (Remove/redesign)  |   (Re-engineer: raise
                         |    price or cut cost)
                         |
                    LOW PROFITABILITY
```

**Action for each quadrant:**
- STARS: Feature prominently, train staff to upsell, never discount
- PLOWHORSES: Reduce portion, swap to cheaper ingredients, raise price 5-10%
- PUZZLES: Better menu placement, staff recommendations, combo with popular items
- DOGS: Remove from menu or completely redesign
