Why this is the realistic ceiling, not a sandbagged floor

April ran ₹10,335/day with zero marketing effort — that is healthy organic. The 5× lift to ₹50K/day end-of-month is everything compounded: ground floor opens day 1 (raises captain AOV ₹110 counter to ₹650 sit-down), 50 reviews unlock Zomato algo by W3, two influencer drops compound through W4, and ad spend stacks only after the funnel that absorbs it is built. We will not hit ₹50K average across 31 days — we climb to it. The math sums to ₹8.94L floor. ₹11L is reachable if execution holds. ₹15L requires aggregator overshoot AND influencer top-of-range AND zero captain absences.

Strategic doctrine

Six rules that govern every decision in May. If any week's plan violates one of these, the plan is wrong, not the rule.

1Familiar destinations only

Every paid click ends in a behavior the customer already knows: order on Zomato/Swiggy, walk into the outlet, place a phone call, navigate Google Maps. WhatsApp ordering is parked. WABA is retention only, not acquisition.

2Dine-in is the hero of May

Packaging upgrade lands in June. Until then, the basket size lives at the table. Ground floor opens May 1 — that single change lifts captain AOV from ₹713 to a target of ₹650+ at higher volume, and converts ₹110 counter takeaway tickets into ₹600 sit-down tickets via outlet credibility.

3Reviews compound, ads don't

50 Zomato reviews unlock default-sort visibility — that is a permanent asset. ₹3,000 of self-orders in May 1–17 buys an algorithm boost that ad spend cannot. We build the asset first, then run ads at the destination it now ranks for.

4Influencer requires lead time

Outreach Day 1. Visit + content shoot Day 8. Reel publishes Day 9. Walk-ins begin Day 10 (24h post-publish), peak Day 13–14. Two drops in May means outreach starts on Day 1 of W1, not "when we feel ready in W3".

5Ads scale only after the funnel that absorbs them is built

Meta Ads at ₹500/day in W2 starts in learning phase — let the algorithm find the converters. Scale to ₹2,500/day only after the top creatives are identified (Day 5–7 of running). Dumping ₹2K/day on Day 1 burns the budget on impressions before the algo knows who buys.

6Halo is the multiplier we don't pay for

A busy outlet at 8pm on a Saturday creates 10–20% lift on counter takeaway and 5–10% lift on aggregator orders the following week — visible on Zomato listing photos, foot-traffic visibility, word-of-mouth. The plan banks halo from W3 onward; without dine-in volume, no halo.

Risk register

What can break this plan, and what we do when it does.

R2 · Self-order ambassador roster falls short of 4 names

Impact: Cannot maintain 5 reviews/day pace. Reviews 50 hits May 22 instead of May 17 — pushes 50-rev unlock and Influencer #2 publish 5 days later, costs ~₹40K of W3 lift. Mitigation: minimum 3 ambassadors gets us to 50 reviews by May 22. If only 2, push timeline to May 26.

R3 · Influencer #1 reel underperforms (<30K reach)

Impact: W2 walk-in lift drops ~₹15–25K. Mitigation: reallocate ₹5K to Influencer #4 micro-tier in W3 to overlap with #2. Treat as creative-fit issue, not platform issue.

R4 · Meta Ads stuck in learning (no exit by Day 8)

Impact: Can't scale ₹500 → ₹2,500. May spend on Meta drops to ₹25K, revenue contribution drops by ₹50–80K. Mitigation: rebuild ad set with broader audience; if still no exit by Day 12, redirect ₹2K/day budget to Zomato Sponsored which is converting reliably.

R5 · Captain absences during W3–W4 peak

Impact: Sit-down service capacity drops. Sat May 24 ₹52K target slips to ₹38–42K. Mitigation: cross-train a counter staff member as backup captain. Ensure SK Muntaz + Faizan + Hardev are scheduled across the May 23–25 weekend.

R6 · Zomato/Swiggy flag self-order pattern
